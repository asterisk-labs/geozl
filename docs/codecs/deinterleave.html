deinterleave

One stream, split into two lanes.

Not a predictor, a split. deinterleave cuts one interleaved stream into two lanes by position. Even indices go to lane 0, odd to lane 1. It is dtype-agnostic, moving elements without ever reading them, so the caller carries any lane meaning itself. The classic use is complex data, keeping the real and imaginary components apart so a spatial predictor downstream never crosses the boundary. It is the inverse of OpenZL's built-in interleave.
